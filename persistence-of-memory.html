Persistence of Memory

Description: Created in 1931 by Salvador Dalí, "The Persistence of Memory" is one of the most iconic works of Surrealism. It features a barren landscape populated by melting clocks draped over strange objects, including a distorted face-like form. This painting challenges our perception of time, suggesting it is fluid, irrelevant, or even an illusion in the dream world. Dalí's haunting imagery evokes subconscious fears and the instability of reality, pushing viewers to confront the surreal nature of their own memories.
