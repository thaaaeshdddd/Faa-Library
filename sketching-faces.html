Introduction

Sketching a human face can be a challenge, but it becomes easier with structure and practice. In this guide, we'll take you step-by-step through proportions, features, and shading techniques tailored for university-level art students.

Materials You'll Need üß∞

  • Sketchbook or smooth drawing paper
  • Graphite pencils (HB, 2B, 4B)
  • Eraser and kneaded eraser
  • Ruler or proportional divider
  • Blending stump or tissue

Facial Proportions üìè

Start with a circle and draw a vertical and horizontal guideline. Divide the face into thirds: hairline to brow, brow to nose base, and nose to chin. Eyes sit one eye-width apart, and ears align with the brow and nose lines.
