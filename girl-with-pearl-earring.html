Girl with a Pearl Earring

Description: Johannes Vermeerâ€™s "Girl with a Pearl Earring," painted around 1665, is often referred to as the "Mona Lisa of the North." This tronie (a study of a character type) features a young girl in exotic dress and a luminous pearl earring, turning over her shoulder with a gaze that is both intimate and enigmatic. The dark background draws focus to her soft features, delicate lighting, and the striking contrast of her blue and gold turban. The painting's quiet beauty and mystery have captivated viewers for centuries.
