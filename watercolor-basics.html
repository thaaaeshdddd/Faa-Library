Introduction

Watercolour painting is one of the most versatile and expressive mediums in art. As a university student, you‚Äôll appreciate how watercolour challenges your planning, patience, and technique. This guide helps you understand how to work with water rather than against it.

Materials You Need üß∞

  • Watercolour paints (pan for travel, tubes for mixing)
  • 300gsm cold-press paper (texture helps absorb paint)
  • Round brushes (sizes 4, 8, and 12)
  • Mixing palette or ceramic plate
  • Two jars of clean water (one to rinse, one to mix)
  • Tissue, sponge, or paper towel for blotting
  • Painters tape or masking fluid (for clean borders)

Fundamental Techniques üñåÔ∏è

  • Wet-on-Wet: Drop colour on damp paper for diffused textures.
  • Wet-on-Dry: Clean edges and layering.
  • Dry Brush: Scratchy texture ideal for foliage, bark, hair.
  • Glazing: Layer translucent colours to build depth.
  • Salt Texture: Sprinkle salt on wet paint to create blooms.
  • Lifting: Use a damp brush or tissue to lift paint from paper.

üìù Smart Tips for Students

  • Always let layers dry completely before adding more colour to avoid muddying.
  • Use a hairdryer to speed up drying when necessary.
  • Mix only two to three colours to keep the painting clean and harmonious.
  • Use thumbnail value sketches to plan your composition and contrast.
  • Practice gradient washes and colour transitions regularly.
