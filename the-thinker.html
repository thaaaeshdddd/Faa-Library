The Thinker is one of the most recognizable sculptures in the world, crafted by the French artist Auguste Rodin in 1904. Originally conceived as part of a larger work called "The Gates of Hell," this bronze masterpiece stands alone as a symbol of deep contemplation and intellectual reflection.

The muscular figure, seated with his chin resting on his hand, is thought to represent the poet Dante. His intense, introspective posture invites viewers to reflect on the power of human thought and emotion.
