üé® Acrylic Painting Tips for Beginners

Introduction

Acrylic painting is incredibly versatile, quick-drying, and beginner-friendly. This tutorial shares essential techniques and tips to help university students build confidence in acrylics.

Materials Needed üß∞

  • Acrylic paints (primary + white and black)
  • Canvas or acrylic paper
  • Brushes (flat, round, fan)
  • Palette and palette knife
  • Water container and cloth
  • Acrylic medium (optional for flow or texture)

Techniques to Master üñåÔ∏è

  • Layering: Let each layer dry before applying the next.
  • Dry Brush: Use a small amount of paint for texture effects.
  • Blending: Blend quickly on canvas as acrylics dry fast.
  • Underpainting: Create depth by starting with a tonal base.
  • Impasto: Apply thick paint with a palette knife for 3D texture.

‚ú® Pro Tips:

  • Use gesso on surfaces to prepare them and prevent paint absorption.
  • Spritz your palette with water to keep paints moist.
  • Clean brushes immediately with water and soap to avoid hardening.
  • Work from background to foreground for better depth control.
  • Mix colors gradually ‚Äî acrylics dry slightly darker.

Practice Exercise:

Try painting a sunset over a mountain. Focus on color blending in the sky and using layers to build the mountain silhouettes. Add texture with a dry brush for the trees in the foreground.
