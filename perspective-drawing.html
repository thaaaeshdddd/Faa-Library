Introduction

Mastering perspective is essential for creating realistic and spatially accurate drawings. Whether you're illustrating architecture or natural landscapes, perspective helps establish depth and form.

Types of Perspective

  • One-Point Perspective: Commonly used in road or hallway scenes, all lines converge at a single vanishing point.
  • Two-Point Perspective: Ideal for corner views, where two sets of lines vanish in different directions.
  • Three-Point Perspective: Used for extreme views, like looking up at a skyscraper or down from above.

Materials You'll Need

  • Pencils (2H, HB)
  • Ruler (for accurate lines)
  • Eraser and sharpener
  • Sketchbook or drawing paper

Step-by-Step Exercise

Try drawing a one-point perspective room:

  • Draw a horizon line and choose a vanishing point.
  • Create a rectangle for the back wall.
  • Connect the corners to the vanishing point to form the room.
  • Add furniture using the same vanishing logic.
